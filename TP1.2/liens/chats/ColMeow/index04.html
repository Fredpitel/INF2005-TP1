Record du monde!

Guinness World Records, the global authority on record-breaking achievement, today revealed its latest record-breaker: Colonel Meow, the Cat with the longest fur.
Colonel Meow, who was adopted as a rescue cat from the Himalayan & Persian Society, needs his fur brushed 2–3 times a week, and it takes both Anne and Eric to complete the job.

Guinness World Records Editor-in-Chief, Craig Glenday, said: “Colonel Meow is being groomed for stardom, and I know the furry feline will be a big hit with readers. He’s an incredible creature, and he’ll undoubtedly inspire people all around to the world to whip out their rulers and start measuring their own cat’s fur.

The Himalayan-Persian cross-breed has fur that reaches 22.87cm (9in) and features in the new Guinness World Records 2014 book out on September 12.
